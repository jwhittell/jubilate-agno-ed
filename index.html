Welcome

Christopher Smart wrote Jubilate Agno during what he called his “jeopardy,” a six-year confinement in St. Luke’s asylum from 1757-1763, but the work remained unpublished until W.F. Stead released a copy under the title Rejoice in the Lamb: A Song from Bedlam in 1939. After its publication, Jubilate Agno was popularized by twentieth-century experimental poets and literary scholars; it is now one of the few works by Smart consistently taken up by the academy. Because recent copies of the text are out of print, and a scholarly edition is lacking, this online edition provides a robust digital access point for academic and casual readers alike.
